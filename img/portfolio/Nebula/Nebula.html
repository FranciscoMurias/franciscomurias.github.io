Nebula Journey (UE4)

Nebula Journey Scene running in realtime in Unreal Engine 4 developed for the 3D Optimization Module for the MA in 3D Games Art.

Project consisted in modeling a custom designed spaceship, and realizing it in engine. The spaceship was built using a modular hard surface workflow.

May 2019

Inspired by space scenes from classic sci fi, as well as the colourful space environments of No Man’s sky, my aim was to design an exterior environment scene of a futuristic capital spaceship navigating through a nebula cloud.

A custom spaceship concept was created in photoshop from kitbashed image examples.

fSpy was used to match the 3D View to the concept image and model a rough blockout.

A modular workflow was used to design each spaceship part and further detail each section.

For the surrounding scenery, a procedural volumetric nebula was created in Unreal Engine's particle and volumetric systems following Epic Game’s Sjoerd De Jong example use of volumetrics and explential height fog.

A volumetric material was created, featuring micro and macro noise variations, brightness controls for the inside and outside of the nebula, and simulated godrays and opacity filters.

A skeletal mesh was used to deform the nebula particle origins so that it could take any desired shape.

For the skybox, a customm space HDRi was created in Spacescape, and a custom skybox blueprint was crated to have individual control over the brightness of the sky and scene.

An asteroid field was procedurally generated around the entire scene with the use of instanced static meshes. This makes it so it only takes 1 draw call to render all the asteroids. The blueprint creates a group of asteroids placed in a defined radius, with a defined size and density range.

The idea of making a portal wormhole emerged as a natural progression of the scene. I wanted the portal to be embedded in the asteroid belt so I sculpted its form from the previous asteroid meshes. For the wormhole effect I used a circle mesh with skewed UVs and a noise material with a panner node to create the spinning VFX.

To simulate the portal effect of looking into a different galaxy, a screen reflection capture projecting into a material was placed on the portal mesh, to simulate the player looking into the portal from any angle.
